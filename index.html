FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Roof repair costs vary depending on the extent of the damage, the type of roofing material, and the size of the area that needs work. Minor repairs like replacing a few shingles are typically affordable, while more extensive damage from storms or leaks may cost more. We offer free estimates so you know exactly what to expect before any work begins.

Common signs of storm damage include missing or curled shingles, dents in gutters or flashing, water stains on your ceiling, and granules collecting in your downspouts. After any significant storm in the Port St. Lucie area, it is a good idea to have a professional inspection to catch damage that may not be visible from the ground.

Most residential roof replacements in Port St. Lucie take one to three days, depending on the size of the roof, the complexity of the job, and weather conditions. Your contractor should give you a clear timeline before work starts so you know what to plan for.

Yes, we can help guide you through the insurance claims process for storm damage. We document the damage thoroughly and work with your insurance company to make the process as smooth as possible. Our goal is to help you get the coverage you are entitled to without the stress of handling it alone.

You should consider a roof inspection if you notice missing or damaged shingles, leaks or water stains inside your home, sagging areas on the roof, or if your roof is over 15 to 20 years old. After a major storm, an inspection is also a good idea even if you do not see obvious damage, since issues can develop over time.

Yes, we provide emergency roof repair and tarping services for situations like active leaks or severe storm damage. When your home is exposed to the elements, acting quickly is critical to prevent further interior damage. Contact us right away and we will respond as fast as possible to protect your home.

Most roofing professionals recommend having your roof inspected once a year, as well as after any major storm. Regular inspections help catch small problems before they turn into costly repairs and can extend the overall life of your roof.

In Florida's hot and storm-prone climate, materials that resist wind, heat, and moisture are ideal. Architectural shingles, metal roofing, and tile are all popular choices in Port St. Lucie because of their durability and ability to withstand high winds. A local roofing professional can help you choose the best option for your home and budget.
