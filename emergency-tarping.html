When Your Roof Can't Wait

There are few things more stressful for a homeowner than watching water pour into your house during a storm. Whether a tree limb has punched through your roof, high winds have ripped away shingles, or an old roof has finally given way, an active roof leak is an emergency that demands immediate action. In those moments, emergency roof tarping is the critical first step to protecting your home and everything in it.

Emergency tarping involves securely installing a heavy-duty waterproof tarp over the damaged area of your roof, creating an immediate barrier against rain and wind. This is not a permanent fix, but it stops the water from continuing to cause damage to your ceilings, walls, insulation, electrical systems, and personal belongings. In Florida's climate, where storms can dump inches of rain in a short period, even a few hours of an uncovered roof breach can result in thousands of dollars in interior damage.

After tarping, we document the damage thoroughly for your insurance company and develop a plan for the permanent repair or replacement. Emergency Tarping Questions

What is emergency roof tarping?

Emergency roof tarping is the installation of a heavy-duty waterproof tarp over a damaged section of your roof to prevent water from entering your home. It is a temporary protective measure used when a permanent repair cannot be made immediately, such as after storm damage or when waiting for materials. The tarp keeps your interior dry until a full repair can be completed.

How quickly can you respond for emergency tarping?

We prioritize emergency tarping requests because we understand that every hour water is entering your home increases the damage and the repair cost. We work to respond as quickly as possible after your call, including evenings and weekends during storm events. Call us directly at (772) 247-8149 for the fastest response.

Does insurance cover emergency tarping?

Most homeowner's insurance policies cover emergency tarping when it is the result of storm damage or another covered event. We document the damage and the tarping work with photos and written records that you can submit with your claim. We can also help guide you through the claims process.
