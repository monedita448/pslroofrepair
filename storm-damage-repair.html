Storm Damage? We Respond Fast.

Living in Port St. Lucie means living with the reality of hurricane season and severe weather. From June through November, tropical systems, strong thunderstorms, and high winds regularly threaten homes throughout the Treasure Coast. When a storm damages your roof, acting quickly is the difference between a straightforward repair and a much more expensive problem involving mold, structural damage, and ruined personal belongings.

Storm damage can take many forms, and some of it is not obvious from the ground. Wind can lift and crease shingles, break the seal that keeps them weatherproof, or rip them off entirely. Hail can crack shingles and bruise the underlying material. Rain can find its way through damaged flashing or gaps around vents and chimneys. Even a storm that does not seem that severe can cause enough subtle damage to create leaks weeks or months later.

We provide thorough storm damage inspections for homeowners in Port St. Lucie, Port Salerno, Stuart, and Fort Pierce. After any significant weather event, we recommend a professional roof inspection even if you do not see obvious damage from the ground. Our team knows exactly what to look for and can document everything with the detailed photos and written reports that insurance companies require.

Dealing with an insurance claim after storm damage can feel overwhelming, especially when you are already dealing with the stress of property damage. We help homeowners navigate the process by documenting the damage thoroughly, meeting with insurance adjusters on-site, and making sure nothing gets overlooked. Our goal is to help you get the coverage your policy provides while getting your roof back to protecting your home as quickly as possible.

If your home has been hit by a storm, do not wait to have the roof inspected. The sooner damage is identified and addressed, the easier and less expensive the repair process will be.

Storm Damage Questions

What should I do immediately after a storm damages my roof?

First, ensure your safety and stay out of any areas with visible structural damage. If water is actively entering your home, we can provide emergency tarping to prevent further interior damage. Then contact your insurance company to start a claim, and schedule a professional roof inspection as soon as possible to document the damage thoroughly.

Do you help with insurance claims for storm damage?

Yes. We document storm damage with detailed photos and written reports that align with what insurance adjusters look for. We can meet with your adjuster on-site and help guide you through the claims process to make sure you receive the coverage you are entitled to under your policy.

Can storm damage go unnoticed for a while?

Yes. Wind damage like lifted or creased shingles may not be visible from the ground, and slow leaks can develop weeks after a storm. That is why a professional inspection after any significant weather event is important, even if your roof appears fine at first glance.
